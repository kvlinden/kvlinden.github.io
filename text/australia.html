What I love most about Australia (Sydney mostly)

  • The scenery is beautiful (e.g., Sydney harbor, the beaches, the Blue Mountains).
  • So is the weather.
  • They have weird animals (e.g., parrots, kangaroos, koalas).
  • They do great barbecues ("barbies" as they would have it).
  • You don’t need to remember first names - just call everyone "mate" - this works with men, women, children and pets.
  • They are fiercely egalitarian (e.g., people tend to ride in the front seats of cabs).
  • They put coconut in everything.
  • They have great shower heads.
  • One of the national TV stations (SBS) features non-Hollywood movies.
  • They have a slightly naughty sense of humor in their advertisements: e.g., "We’re #1 on #2" (an ad for a waste treatment company), "we protect your largest organ longer" (an ad for sunscreen with a picture of naked man holding a bottle of the product in a strategic location), and "Try freezing your nuts this summer" (an add for frozen snickers bars).
  • Primary and Secondary students ride free on public transport.
  • Crime is lower (e.g., We’ve had a number of lost items returned that would have been long gone in the US or England).
  • The seafood is excellent and affordable.
  • It’s thousands of miles from anywhere.

What I hate most about Australia

  • Sunburn - No one gets a tan, it’s a burn or nothing.
  • You’ll eventually be killed by either a poisonous animal or a rip tide (or skin cancer).
  • Santa shouldn’t wear shorts.
  • They’ve reduced all the nouns in the English language to two classes, those ending in "ie" and those ending in "o". Here are some translations:
    • barbie - barbecue
    • mozzie - mosquito
    • prezzie - present
    • garbo - garbage
    • muso - musician
    • arvo - afternoon
    • and so on - ad nauseum
